VisitingLandmarks is an open source app I have been building as part of the Coding Da vinci Nord Hackathon. It is a game for discovering landmark buildings based on open data from The Palaces and Culture Agency of Protected and Listed Buildings in Denmark (FBB).

Denmark has about 9000 listed buildings and the Danish state spend a lot of money and resources protecting them. With this app we wanted to raise awareness of the buildings and encourage people to engage more with their cultural and architectural heritage. It will soon be a mobile app!

Sounddrop.audio is a website and mobile app that allows you to link sound recordings to places. SoundDrop makes it possible to upload a sound recording with picture, title and location, while also encouraging users to discover sound recordings near them on a digital map, currently using SoundCloud to upload and save sound recordings.

SoundDrop is not just a cultural product but also an open source tool to create more diversity in the tech community. Our participants learn by working with the app to program. SoundDrop was created in 2014 as part of the Rails Girls Summer of Code and since then seven women have worked on it.

FrauenLoop is an organisation that offers coaching, counseling and hands-on web development courses to women. The topics in the introductory courses include Ruby on Rails, JavaScript & SQL, workshops on UI and UX basics, web and business analytics and resume prep. I volunteer as a Ruby on Rails coach and built the organisations website together with Wissam Ghozlan.
